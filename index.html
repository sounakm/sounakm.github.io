About Me

I am a 4th year graduate student at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Princeton University. I am working on color centers in diamond for quantum networks, supervised by Prof. Nathalie de Leon. My research interest lies in optics and spectroscopy as a means to understand quantum systems such as atom-like defects in diamond. I am currently exploring new defects in diamond which are promising candidates for quantum technologies.


I completed BS-MS from the Department of Physical Sciences, Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Kolkata, India. As a part of my MS thesis under the guidance of Prof. Nirmalya Ghosh, I investigated the orbital Hall effect of light along with polarization studies.

Research



New Defects in Diamond

My current research interest is to use photoluminescence spectroscopy techniques to look at new color centers in diamond as potential candidates for quantum network and sensing applications

Electron Spin Resonance

We developed a home-built pulsed electron spin resonance (ESR) setup for investigating coherence properties of new defects at cryogenic to room temperatures

Telecom O-band Emitter in Diamaond

Recently, we reported a new silicon-related defect in diamond that emits in the telecom O-band

Silicon Vacancy

I am also investigating single neutron silicon vacancy centers (SiV0) in diamond with magneto-optic spectrosocopy for quantum network applications
